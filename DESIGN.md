# Cyril Liu Website Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

International editorial profile meets PMO methodology workbench, with an AI liquid-glass atmosphere. The design should feel like a refined professional feature story: calm, intelligent, structured, and human. Use translucent glass surfaces, slow liquid refraction, subtle purple-blue inference fields, and operational signals such as timelines, nodes, coordinates, margin notes, and routing lines. Avoid stock-office photography, generic SaaS dashboards, oversized empty hero slogans, loud purple gradients, and decorative blobs that feel unrelated to the work.

## Colors

- Paper: `#f3f0ea`
- Ink: `#111827`
- Muted Ink: `#5f6673`
- Rule: `#c9c1b4`
- Signal Blue: `#2f6df6`
- Intelligence Violet: `#8b5cf6`
- Liquid Lilac: `#c4b5fd`
- Deep Graphite: `#171a1f`
- Warm White: `#fffaf2`

## Typography

- Primary: system sans-serif stack for crisp multilingual rendering.
- Editorial headings: large but measured, with generous line-height and no negative letter spacing.
- Labels: small uppercase or compact Chinese labels with restrained tracking only where readable.

## Motion

- Background motion must be slow, loopable, and non-distracting.
- Use liquid-glass refraction, soft violet-blue fields, line draws, node pulses, and soft parallax-like movement.
- Website interactions use 180-280ms transitions and transform/opacity only.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not use stock office photos as the main visual.
- Do not make the site look like a SaaS landing page or server product page.
- Do not use card-heavy nested dashboards in the hero.
- Do not rely on bright gradients or neon tech aesthetics.
- Do not leave navigation or CTA links empty.
